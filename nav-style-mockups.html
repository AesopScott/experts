Navigation style study

Ten professional treatments for the grouped nav.

The layout stays fixed. Each option changes color, density, borders, shape, and contrast so we can choose a style that feels like 25experts instead of a mockup.

Same structure in every option Vertical side labels, four groups, two-by-two buttons, brand on the left. These are style candidates only. The current live nav can be updated once a direction is chosen.
1. Quiet AesopClosest to the existing site: warm white, green active state, restrained accents.
Best default
FeelCalm, familiar, nonprofit professional.
StrengthLeast disruptive to current pages.
RiskStill a little soft.
2. Dark AuthorityA premium dark shell with light buttons and a stronger institutional tone.
Most serious
FeelEditorial, high-trust, more executive.
StrengthVery clear nav boundary.
RiskHeavier than the rest of the site.
3. Utility GridSharper, flatter, and more product-like with fewer decorative signals.
Most app-like
FeelOperational and precise.
StrengthScans quickly.
RiskLess warm.
4. Foundation WarmthUses a controlled gold tint to make the nonprofit mission feel more present.
Most nonprofit
FeelTrustworthy, generous, donor-friendly.
StrengthWarmer mission signal.
RiskCan skew too foundation-brochure.
5. Crisp ProfessionalWhite card, blue-green mark, compact corners, and stronger button contrast.
Cleanest
FeelConsulting-grade, clean, restrained.
StrengthFeels polished fast.
RiskLess distinctive.
6. Soft PlatformA smoother product feel with rounded boxes and green active buttons.
Most approachable
FeelFriendly, modern, less institutional.
StrengthInviting for patrons.
RiskCan feel less serious.
7. Editorial RuleSquare edges and black active states for a publication-like system.
Most editorial
FeelMagazine, archive, standards board.
StrengthVery disciplined.
RiskCould feel cold.
8. Rounded TrustPill-like buttons and a round mark, but still restrained enough for the theme.
Softest
FeelAccessible and human.
StrengthGood for broad audiences.
RiskMay be too casual.
9. Knowledge SystemCooler, more academic, with the blue used as a restrained structural accent.
Most academic
FeelLibrary, curriculum, knowledge base.
StrengthProfessional without being dark.
RiskCooler than current brand.
10. Boardroom AccentMostly neutral with a single gold active state for a more executive feel.
Most executive
FeelBoardroom, donors, senior leaders.
StrengthConfident and mature.
RiskGold may compete with green identity.
