How scholarships work

Scholarships are not a separate experience. They open the same platform.

A scholarship patron should be able to participate in the same expert communities as a paid patron. The difference is how the access is funded.

Who scholarships are for

Access should go where practical AI literacy changes real options.

The scholarship program is designed for people who would benefit from structured AI learning but may not be ready to pay for expert access.

1Youth and students
Young learners who need early exposure to practical, trustworthy AI education.
2Working professionals
People whose jobs are changing and who need applied AI skills to keep moving forward.
3Community cohorts
Libraries, workforce programs, schools, and nonprofits that can organize learners locally.
4High-intent learners
People who will use access actively: watching, attending, asking, building, and sharing.
