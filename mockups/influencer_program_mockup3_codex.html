Creator questions

The objections we should answer upfront.

Influencers will not join because the page is pretty. They join when the risk feels understood and the mission feels credible.

Do I have to leave YouTube, Patreon, Substack, or X?

No. The program is non-exclusive. Keep your existing channels and use Experts as the deeper learning and patron experience layer.

Why does the nonprofit structure matter?

It changes the incentives. The platform is built to grow AI education and audience trust, not maximize shareholder profit. Creator payouts and programming support are part of the mission.

Who owns my content?

You retain ownership. The platform receives a non-exclusive license to host, stream, recommend, and package approved content according to the creator agreement.

What if my patrons do not migrate?

The migration plan should include a founding patron offer, personalized landing page, ready-to-send copy, and optional limited guarantee for the first anchor creators.

How does the marketing or Growth Fund work?

The recommended formula is 50% attributed paid patrons, 25% net new patron growth, and 25% engagement and retention. This rewards size, growth, and quality.

How does ranking work?

Ranking should use transparent signals like views, completion, saves, comments, follows gained, workshop attendance, freshness, and human review. AI assists discovery; it should not be an opaque judge.

Can for-profit companies influence what gets featured?

No. Sponsors and advertisers may support the platform, but programming and curation decisions must be mission-led and kept distinct from commercial relationships.
